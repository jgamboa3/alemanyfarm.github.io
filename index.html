Growing Food and Community in San Francisco

Alemany Farm is San Francisco's largest agricultural site. It offers a place to grow food, learn practical skills, care for the land, and share fresh produce with the community.

Welcome to Alemany

This nonprofit urban farm brings together neighbors, volunteers, and students through food growing, education, and environmental care.

Friends of Alemany Farm manages a 3.5 acre organic farm ecosystem in southeast San Francisco, supporting food security, environmental education, and community care.

Produce grown at the farm is shared free of charge with neighbors, volunteers, The Free Farm Stand, and other community partners.

Volunteer workdays, workshops, and internships give people hands-on experience in urban agriculture and community stewardship.

Impact at the Farm

These 2025 numbers show the scale of the farm's growing, volunteer, and education programs.

35,500 Pounds

In 2025, the farm grew an estimated 35,500 pounds of fresh fruits and vegetables for free community distribution.

14,000 Volunteer Hours

The farm recorded more than 14,000 hours of volunteer time across 99 community workdays in 2025.

20 Workshops

Alemany Farm offered 20 workshops for more than 400 participants, with no one turned away for lack of funds.
